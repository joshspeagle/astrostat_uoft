People

The ART is made up of researchers across astrophysics, statistics, and computer science spanning a wide range of career stages. More information about individual members of the group can be found below.


Faculty

Gwendolyn Eadie

Gwen is an Assistant Professor of Astrostatistics, jointly appointed between the David A. Dunlap Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics (51%) and the Department of Statistical Sciences (49%). Her research interests includes hierarchical Bayesian inference, generalized linear models, spatial point processes, and time series analysis for the study of the Milky Way Galaxy, ultra-diffuse galaxies, star clusters and globular clusters, stars and stellar flares, and fast radio bursts.

Josh Speagle

Josh is an Assistant Professor in Statistical Sciences & Astronomy and Astrophysics. His work focuses on using a combination of astronomy, statistics, data science, and AI to analze massive datasets containing billions of stars and galaxies to understand how galaxies like our own Milky Way form, behave, and evolve over time. https://joshspeagle.com/

Phil Van-Lane

Phil is a 2nd year PhD student in Astronomy & Astrophysics co-supervised by Drs. Eadie and Speagle, and Dr. Ryan Cloutier from McMaster University. His research focuses on leveraging machine learning and statistical methods to develop stellar dating methods using rotation and magnetic activity data, with a focus on M dwarfs. His long-term goal is to build a catalogue of M dwarf ages, and use these constraints to inform exoplanet evolutionary analyses. He previously completed his BSc at University of Waterloo in Earth Sciences with a Geophysics specialization, then worked in engineering consulting, software development, data engineering, and product management roles for several years before beginning his PhD in Fall 2022.
