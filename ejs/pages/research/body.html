Research

Statistics and data are integral parts of many areas of modern astronomy, and so the ART is conducting research in a broad range of areas across astrophysics, statistics, and data science. Information on some of the areas and topics we are currently investigating, along with the awesome people involved, can be found below.

Fast Radio Bursts and the Transient Sky

Covering fast radio bursts, transients, and other areas. (Image credit: Danielle Futselaar/artsource.nl.)

The Mysteries of Dark Matter

Covering dark matter probes through ultra-diffuse galaxies, distribution function modelling, the galaxy-halo connection, large-scale structure, Milky Way dynamics, and other areas. (Image credit: ESA/Hubble.)

People involved: Gwen Eadie, Josh Speagle, Tanveer Karim, David Li, Anika Slizewski, Huanqing Chen, Ioana Zelko


Ancient Galaxies and Star Clusters

Covering globular clusters, quiescent galaxies, and other areas. (Image credit: ESA/Hubble & NASA.)

The Structure, Formation, and Evolution of Galaxies

Covering how galaxies form, assemble, evolve, and look, plus everything in-between. (Image credit: NASA, ESA, CSA, and STScI.)

Covering topics related to physics-informed, probabilistic, robust, and interpretable ML methods, along with other areas. (Image credit: 3blue1brown.)
