Research

Statistics and data are integral parts of many areas of modern astronomy, and so the ART is conducting research in a broad range of areas across astrophysics, statistics, and computer science. Information on some of the areas and topics we are currently investigating can be found below. Please check back every once in a while since this collection changes over time!


Fast Radio Bursts and the Transient Sky

Covering fast radio bursts, transients, and other similar areas.


The Mysteries of Dark Matter

Covering dark matter probes through ultra-diffuse galaxies, distribution function modelling, galaxy-halo connection, large-scale structure, Milky Way dynamics, and similar areas.


Ancient Star Clusters and Galaxies

Covering globular clusters and quiescent galaxies.


The Structure, Formation, and Evolution of Galaxies

Covering galaxy assembly, chemodynamical modelling, Galactic structure, dwarf galaxies, galaxy morphology, galaxy evolution, and other similar areas.


The Secret Life of Stars

Covering star formation, open clusters, stellar activity, stellar evolution, and similar areas.


Scalable Inference with Large Datasets

Covering sampling methods, hierarchical inference, data-driven approaches, data integration, and similar areas.


Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)

Covering topics related to physics-informed, probabilistic, robust, and interpretable ML methods, along with similar areas.
